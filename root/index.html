Experience

Most Recent First

SVT Robotics

Automation at the Speed of Need

Our infrastructure at SVT has two main parts. First is the SOFTBOT Studio. The Studio is our cloud platform where customers can orchestrate systems of robotics and automation technology however they need to accomplish their goals. After some configuration this orchestration gets converted into a package of several containers (SOFTBOTs) responsible for the orchestration of singular pieces of technology. These containers can be pushed or manually downloaded and comprise of the second part: the SOFTBOT runtime. This runtime is what actually does the business of managing the various technologies our customers require. Together these two parts come together to create the first codeless robotics automation platform.

Before we stood up the SOFTBOT platform there were multiple projects we completed as professional services. Essentially we offered our expertise to get some initial customers and build a name for SVT while designing and building the architecture for the mass undertaking that is the SOFTBOT platform and runtime.

Validic

Powering Healthcare with Data

My first duty at Validic was to complete the next phase of a project already in progress. This project was part of professional services we offered and was unrelated to Validic's core products. The company we were hired by developed a blood glucose meter with self-contained cartridges that pricked your skin and tested your blood glucose levels all in one package without the need for a full lancer and test strip kit. We were responsible for buliding the diabetes monitoring platform they wanted to pair with their new device.

A large part of Validic's business is Inform, a healthcare platform for streaming personal health data. This data is sourced via integrations with nearly 500 digital health technologies. I worked on the Impact team which utilized Inform to build a remote patient monitoring platform. One of my larger successes on this team was implementing a middleware layer that could handle SSO connections using SAML and OIDC so we could embed Impact into Electronic Health Record (EHR) systems.

Dude Solutions

Operations Management Software for Smarter Operations

Per their tagline, Dude Solutions builds operations management software. My time at Dude Solutions was mostly spent learning the ins and outs of design and implementation of RESTful APIs in both Node and .NET (Framework and later Core). I was also introduced to containerization through Docker and even spearheaded an effort to Dockerize our Node services since at the time we only had our .NET services containerized. Another Node related initiative I undertook was building our first services (in Node) with WebSockets. Our primary uses for WebSockets were real-time updates and alerts for concurrent resource access.

I also spent time working on internal tooling for our customer support team. We automated our new client process which involved some fairly painful legacy SalesForce integrations! Other valuable tools include an internal SAML configuration tool usable by our support staff so our engineers weren't constantly contacted just to add a new configuration and client impersonation tools for live support sessions.
